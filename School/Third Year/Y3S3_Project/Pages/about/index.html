Meet the team

Connor: Age 22

My name is Connor LaGuff, I am currently completing my third year of schooling for my Electronic Systems Engineering Course at Conestoga College. I have had many different experiences over many jobs. Through my programs Co-ops I have developed necessary skills for creating, documenting, and implementing solutions for any applications need. I thoroughly enjoy working and love a job that really makes me think. Working is a passion for me and I believe that I have an impeccable work ethic that never backs down from any job no matter the level of difficultly. I am kind, caring, and outgoing. I work well on my own but also excel in team situations.


Graeme: Age 21

My name is Graeme Judge, and I am in my third year of electronics system engineering (Honours B. Eng) at Conestoga College. Throughout the past three years I have developed both the technical and practical skills involved in electronic hardware design as well as circuit analysis. Additionally, I have developed a strong background in both computer and embedded systems programming and have applied these skills in several hardware and software projects. Such projects included motion control platform development focused on the hardware design application. This involved interfacing with many devices such as motor drivers, communication ports, LEDs, and other peripheral devices to control the movement and general operation of a remote-controlled robot platform. Regarding the software aspect, I designed a coded messaging system able to communicate between two computers via both encrypted text messages and audio recordings using an RS232 connection with a frequency-shift keying modulator and de-modulator


Sean: Age 22

Currently in my third year at Conestoga in the Electronic Systems Engineering program. I have participated in studies of electrical theory, advanced mathematics, telecommunications, programming, machine learning and engineering standards of practice. I am particularly interested in the manufacturing of various electronics and how their functionality affects everyday life and industry. I have worked several part-time and co-op positions at companies such as ACRP Ltd and Waterloo Regional Police Service where teamwork and coordination are needed to be successful and productive. I have also done volunteer work helping families who live with the effects of autism. My ability to communicate and work with others in combination with my desire for knowledge will help me to be a successful worker. Ultimately, I intend to complete my Electronics Engineering degree at Conestoga College and work towards obtaining my Professional Engineering license.
